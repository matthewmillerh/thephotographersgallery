Lindeka Gloria Qampi

View Gallery

Born in 1969 in Bolotwa, South Africa. Lives and works in Cape Town, South Africa.

In the mid 2000s, self-taught photographer Lindeka Qampi made a living off selling postcards. She used her own images, found her own outlets and focused her content on her immediate surroundings: daily township life.

Qampi’s work captures the most significant aspects of daily township life — of which she is a part — from the private sphere to the euphoria of play. She has documented everyday scenes from different townships around Cape Town, interrogating a variety of issues: limited spaces around land as an issue for all, different cultures, creativity amongst people, awareness of cultural norms, special moments, the value of a shared history, and, most recently, township fashion.

Most importantly, Qampi explores these encounters of daily life to express the poetics and politics of the ‘ordinary act’ and therein the potential to begin imagining new possibilities of the future. Through this intimate lens, the nuances of human interaction shed a more complex light on the greater changes taking place (or not taking place) in South Africa.
