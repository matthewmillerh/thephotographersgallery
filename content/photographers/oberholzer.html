Obie Oberholzer

View Gallery

Born in 1947. Lives and works in Nature's Valley, South Africa.

For over three decades, Obie Oberholzer has been travelling the dusty roads of Africa. If asked about his own photography, he would merely shrug, smile and then tell you about some funny incident that happened somewhere on the road. He has enthusiastically pursued his travel dreams across the continent, reflecting extraordinary people, places and encounters along the way.

Without ever incorporating any of the globally influential photographic styles from Europe and North America, he has remained true to his own brand of visual language that ties in so deeply with the African landscape that forms the backbone of his photographs. Consequently, Oberholzer's distinctive scenes of travel resonate with a recognisable dynamic energy; his images capture the essence of spontaneity in moments that are usually missed in the blink of an eye.

Willem Boshoff describes Oberholzer as an "intergalactic traveller and lone planet wandererâ€¦ on a quest to uncover an extraordinary brand of bliss for the world to behold." Oberholzer uses camera angle to great effect in creating this "extraordinary brand of bliss," where, for example, aerial perspective flattens the crests of waves to what appears to be the same level as the dunes that roll down to meet them.

These rich landscapes of his are not spaces that are directly inhabited by people, but rather seem to be devoid of human life entirely. They are spaces that Oberholzer passed through on his "quest to uncover" the hidden everyday things that he aims to render wondrous for "the world to behold."

Oberholzer has held more than 35 local solo exhibitions and various international solos in Germany, The Netherlands, USA, Spain and Poland since 1975.
