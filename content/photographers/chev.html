Erik Chevalier

View Gallery

Born in Rome. Lives and works in Sardinia, Italy.

Chevalier is a painter and a photographer who has lived and worked in Ghana, Denmark and Canada before settling in Sardinia. His photographic work explores issues ranging from ethno-anthropological suggestions to contemporary history; including displacement, migration and alienation. His acclaimed series entitled The Family Safe is a faux family album for the average Westener and investigates the hypothetical photo album genre. Chevalier constructed his new realities and memories using both old family photographs and found images.

Chevalier is active in his adoptive home, Sardinia, particularly as a contributing photographer and editor to the quarterly magazine, Fuortima.

He researches a wide range of photographic spaces, from ethno-anthropological suggestions to contemporary history. He sees himself as a nomadic artist, and likes to explore dreams and anxieties of the human mind.
