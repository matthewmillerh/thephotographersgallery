Lian Zhiping

View Gallery

Born in Fujian, China. Lives and works in China and Berlin, Germany.

Zhiping's work is based on personal experience and often comments on ordinary life in all its complexities. Most of her images are constructed; she uses photography to create her own utopian (or dystopian) worlds.

For her 2010 series, Islands, Zhiping photographed all the clothes that she had worn that summer on a bed inside her home. This was a deliberate attempt to isolate these items; to create an island of pieces of clothing. Worn mostly outside during the summer, these clothes would only continue to exist within the limited space of the photographs, in start contrast to their impermanent and unencumbered previous existence.

Zhiping's inspiration lies in her quest to utilize her photography to comment on the inherent vulnerability of human beings: a vulnerability that universally co-exists with complex emotion, dreams and all the predicaments of reality.
